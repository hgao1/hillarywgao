'9 Pigeons'

On the stage, in a line stands 9 pigeons.
From left to right their names are:
9, 7, 5, 3, 1, 2, 4, 6, and 8.
In the world of the play, they are standing in a “V” shape because they are a flock of birds.
It is also the easiest for each character to hear the characters closest to them,
and hardest to hear ones who are physically furthest.
They think they are flying south to Cancun for vacation.
