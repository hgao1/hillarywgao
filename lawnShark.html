lawnShark is a mobile, hands-off, mini lawn mower. It is powered by a rechargeable battery,
and its chasis is completely 3D printed (plastic!).
This is intended to be a movement towards cheaper material that's easier to source and find.
In addition, the entire code is meant to be completely open-source,
it is its own lesson into how to design + build something of the sort (and my first lesson into doing so).
Here are the goals & steps:
  • Can move in a programmable way (arduino, battery controlled)
  • Is able to cut grass (perhaps the most important goal)
  • Can cover a small (nyc-sized) lawn
  • Use ultrasound sensors to stop when something is in the way
  • Swap ultrasound with ML to identify when someone's in 'frame' (v2.0)
The set-up involved:
  • a microcontroller (Arduino)
  • 12V DC Motor
  • TT Hobby Motors (4)
  • Motor Drivers (L298N)
  • OVO ultrasound
  • Breadboard, wires (for prototyping!)
The total cost of which was a bit more, around $100.
This is, maybe a lot, but lawn Roombas are (according to my mom) around $900.
